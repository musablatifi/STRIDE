The Challenge

Hack for GAZA is more than a hackathon‚Äîit's a movement.

We're calling on engineering students and young professionals to apply their technical skills toward solving critical humanitarian challenges. Inspired by the ongoing crisis in Gaza, where infrastructure, homes, communication systems, and basic services have been severely damaged, this hackathon focuses on developing practical, scalable engineering solutions.

Your ideas and innovations can help rebuild communities, restore safety, and create sustainable solutions for areas affected by humanitarian crises worldwide.

Participation Guidelines

Eligibility

  • Open to all engineering students and young professionals
  • Teams of 2-5 members
  • Both Indian and international participants welcome

Rules

  • All code must be original (open-source libraries allowed)
  • Teams must work within designated timeframe
  • Solutions must be feasible and practical
  • Proper documentation required
  • Presentation to judges mandatory

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

All engineering students and young professionals, regardless of branch or year.

Is there a registration fee?

Free for STRIDE members, nominal fee for non-members to cover operational costs.

Can I participate alone?

Teams are encouraged, but solo participation is allowed.

What tools/platforms can we use?

Any technology stack, framework, or platform is allowed.

Will mentors be available during the hackathon?

Yes, industry mentors will be available for guidance throughout the event.

How do we submit our project?

Submission details will be shared before the hackathon starts.

What if my solution is incomplete?

Submit what you have - partial solutions are accepted and judged fairly.

Can international students participate?

Yes, the hackathon is open to all international participants.
